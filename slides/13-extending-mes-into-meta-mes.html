Section II-B: Meta-MES

Extending MES into Meta-MES

Semiconductor production is too dynamic for static scheduling alone.

Why Traditional Planning Struggles

  • Complex flow: fabs coordinate hundreds of tightly coupled process steps.
  • Recipe sensitivity: small changes can alter throughput, wear, and quality.
  • Unexpected events: breakdowns, maintenance tasks, and new orders disrupt static plans.

What Meta-MES Adds

  • Production-line reconfiguration when conditions change.
  • Resource management and advanced scheduling driven by factory state.
  • A manufacturing operating system for monitoring, control, and custom applications.
Meta-MES consumes AI outputs such as punctual anomalies, faulty-wafer distribution metrics, and remaining-time-to-failure estimates.
