Part III: Preliminary Study

Real-Time Windowing Strategy

Moving from offline inspection to in-situ monitoring.

The Sliding Window (W)

Instead of the entire 176s recording, the ML model is trained to reconstruct a small time-window W.

  • Stride: 1 second (matching sensor resolution).
  • Recon: Computes the error only up to the current timestamp $t_0$.
  • Alert: If $RE > Threshold$ at $t_0$, an immediate alert is sent to Meta-MES.

Why "Windowed"?

Punctual anomaly detection requires seeing the *evolution* of the signal, not just a single data point. A window captures the temporal context needed to identify mechanical wear or gas flow instability as it happens.

This architecture allows SMART-IC to flag faults in-situ, potentially stopping a process before it destroys the wafer.
