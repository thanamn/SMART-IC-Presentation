Section II-A: AI-Powered Engine

Why Multivariate Deep Learning

Digitalized fabs generate rich traces that traditional single-variable analysis struggles to exploit.

Traditional FD/FC Limits

  • Univariate view: many methods still monitor one variable at a time.
  • Weak on dependencies: interactions across sensors are easily missed.
  • De-correlation tricks: often difficult to justify on real production data.

SMART-IC Approach

  • Multivariate traces: deep learning processes many sensors jointly over time.
  • Latent structure: hidden temporal and cross-sensor patterns can be learned directly.
  • One engine, many tasks: the same paradigm supports diagnosis, forecasting, and generation.

Section II-A positions deep learning as the key tool for extracting knowledge from complex semiconductor traces.
