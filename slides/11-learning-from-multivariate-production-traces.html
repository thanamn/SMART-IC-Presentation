Section II-A: AI-Powered Engine

Learning from Multivariate Production Traces

Digitalized fabs generate rich multivariate traces that traditional single-variable analysis struggles to exploit.

Traditional FD/FC Limits

  • Univariate view: many methods still monitor one variable at a time.
  • Weak on dependencies: interactions across sensors are easily missed.
  • De-correlation tricks: often difficult to justify on real production data.

SMART-IC Approach

SMART-IC applies deep learning to multivariate traces so temporal patterns and cross-sensor dependencies can be learned directly. The same engine can then support diagnosis, forecasting, and data generation.

Multivariate analysis preserves both temporal evolution and cross-sensor relationships.

Section II-A positions deep learning as the key tool for extracting knowledge from complex semiconductor traces.
